If this is new to you

I am a Catholic, this is my first time coming to an EF mass
The Extraordinary Form of the Roman Church's Latin Rite had developed organically since the early days of Christianity during the days of the Church Fathers. Over the centuries it acquired plentiful bounties of beautiful traditions from various communities and locales. In the 16th century as a response to the Protestant Reformation, Pope St Pius V codified formally the various diversities in the liturgy into what was since then used for the next 500 years until the 1960s. Development had since the infancy of Christianity been organic - a gradual growth and trimming much akin to the growing and cutting of hair.

Thankfully, depsite the brash suppression of many spiritually edifying observations, the structure of the new mass had remained generally intact. If you recall the structure of the Ordinary Form of the mass that you are familiar with, you will be able to follow along without much difficulties. Do pick up a copy of the mass booklet, you should find the translations useful. You will also find in it prayers of a poetic splendour unseen since the dark days following the 2nd Vatican Council.

If you don't feel comfortable attending on your own, do feel free to bring along a friend who may be interested!
I am a Christian, first time at a Catholic mass
The Catholic mass is the source and summit of a Christian's spiritual life. It is no mere reenactment nor is it a community feast. It is the one and only true sacrifice of Christ. The Holy Mass, through the mystery of the Triunal God who exists outside of time and space, is united with the one Crucifixion of Christ. And in so doing it shares in the infinite graces and merits warranted by that greatest act in all of creation. Christ died once and for all, the Holy Mass is not a re-crucifixion of Christ.

In this mass you will witness a form of liturgy that had been celebrated for two millenium by the Catholic Church, developing gradually and organically over the centuries. It traces its roots to the Apostles. Unfortunately the Catholic Church went through a major crisis in the revolutionary decade of the 1960s. The Church is still in the depths of this crisis - a spirtual one as a result of the abandonment of orthodox (small-'o') Catholic spirituality inspired by God for innovations invented by man.

If you have Catholic friends who are willing to join you in attending our celebrations, you are most welcome to invite them along! Please do indicate so in the RSVP. If you prefer to find out more before making your decision, feel free to contact us too.
I am a non-Christian, first time at a Catholic mass
The Catholic Mass is the source and summit of a Christian's life. Through the Triunal God who exists outside of space and time, each and every Catholic mass around the world, throughout history, is united with the one and only Crucifixion of Christ atop Calvary. There is only one sacrifice. But its merits is infinite, and all Catholic masses are united with it.

Do not worry if this is your first visit to a Catholic mass. You may simply sit and observe the solemnity, or stand as the situation calls for it. You may choose not to kneel if you do not feel inclined to. While the church is a dignified and somber place, do relax and make yourself comfortable! God loves each and every one of you, and is delighted at your presence.

Sometimes when we attend a social function among a community we are not familiar with, we find having a friend coming together helps very much in alleviating the awkwardness. You are most welcome to bring a friend along no matter if this friend is a Catholic or if this is equally unfamiliar to him or her. Feel free to contact us if you need more details before making a decision!
